Work and Lab

Two shelves. Work is program systems built for teacher preparation, described in general terms. Lab is what I build for fun, which is how the Work shelf got its skills.

Shelf 01 / Work

Program systems

  • Instrument design

    Standards-aligned quarterly progress reporting

    Parallel progress-monitoring instruments for two special education licensure tracks, each traceable item by item to state licensure standards, with quarter-based progression built in. Full case study.

  • Coaching systems

    Candidate coaching model and cadence

    A coaching structure for apprentices and residents built around High Leverage Practices, with a regular observation and debrief cycle designed to fit inside a mentor teacher's real schedule.

  • Placement design

    Mentor selection and placement tooling

    Structured documentation that helps districts and programs match apprentices to the right mentor teachers, because a bad placement is the most expensive mistake an apprenticeship program can make.

  • Grants and reporting

    Federal grant narrative and reporting support

    Writing and systems work supporting federally funded teacher preparation, where the reporting requirements are their own engineering problem.

Shelf 02 / Lab

Built for fun, kept because useful

  • Self-hosted

    Home infrastructure

    A small self-hosted stack: NAS, a dozen-plus Docker services for media, photos, books, and feeds, published through a zero-trust tunnel, with a smart home layer running on Home Assistant.

  • App

    Ranked-choice voting for a movie club

    A React app plus a Google Apps Script tallier that runs real ranked-choice elections so my movie club argues about films instead of about voting systems.

  • Tooling

    Receipt scanning for YNAB

    A small vision-model-powered tool that turns grocery receipts into split transactions in my budget, because typing out a Costco run is a life tax I refuse to pay.

  • Games

    Stellaris modding

    Paradox script work: merging conflicting mods, linting mod files, and maintaining a curated Steam Workshop collection.
